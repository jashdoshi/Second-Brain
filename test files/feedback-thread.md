Marcus,

Thanks for getting the logo files over. Diane and I spent time with them this morning and have consolidated our feedback below. Overall reaction is genuinely positive — this is a meaningful step forward from where we started and Tomás has clearly understood what we were after.

**What's working:**
The wordmark refinement is exactly right. The weight adjustment makes it feel more considered without losing any of the brand recognition equity we've built. The secondary mark — the angled "M" device — is something we weren't expecting and we actually love it. It gives us a lot more flexibility for small-format applications (favicon, hangtags, etc.) which has been a real gap in our current brand system.

The monochrome variant is clean and will work well for our private label packaging for *Meridian Home*.

**What needs attention:**

1. **Color application on the primary logo:** We're seeing a color that doesn't match what's in the draft brand guidelines document (page 7, "Primary Warm Ivory" — PMS 9183 C). The logo file appears to be using a slightly cooler off-white. Is this an intentional departure or a file error? We want to make sure what Tomás has in his working files matches the guidelines spec before we approve.

2. **Spacing on the stacked lockup:** The secondary mark sits too close to the wordmark in the vertical/stacked version. At small sizes — think a 1" print application — the two elements visually merge. We'd like to see the clearance increased by what feels like roughly 20–25%.

3. **The script weight feels slightly heavy for digital:** We've been mocking the logo up against some of the OOH treatments Tomás shared in the August 14th internal review (Diane saw those through you) and on digital screens it reads as slightly bolder than intended. This might just be a rendering issue, but worth confirming with a screen-optimized export.

Nothing here is a major rework — these all feel like refinements. Let us know when we can expect a revised version.
