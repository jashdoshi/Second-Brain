*[Call begins at 10:03 AM after connection issues resolved]*

**Marcus Linde:** Alright, we're recording now. Welcome everyone — really glad to have both of you here. Diane, Jeff, we've been looking forward to this since our intro call last month. I'll hand it over to Claire to kick us off on the strategy side.

**Claire Whitmore:** Thanks Marcus. So before we dive into any creative territory, I want to make sure we really understand where Meridian is right now and where you want to be. Diane, you mentioned in your brief that the brand has started to feel "stuck." Can you unpack that a bit?

**Diane Castellano:** Sure. So we've been operating with essentially the same brand identity since 2017. At the time it was a great fit — we were a value-focused home goods retailer and that positioning made sense. But over the last three years we've really pushed upmarket. Our average basket size is up 34%, we've opened four new flagship stores in premium retail corridors, and yet our branding still reads like a budget brand. It's creating this bizarre disconnect for new customers who find us through editorial coverage and then feel let down when they see our logo or open one of our emails.

**Jeff Purdy:** It's the first impression problem. We lose people in the gap between what the product is and what the brand signals.

**Claire Whitmore:** That's a really useful framing, Jeff. Who do you see as your primary competitive set right now?

**Jeff Purdy:** Honestly it's shifted. Two years ago we'd have said HomeBase and Crate & Collective. Now we're seeing ourselves more in conversation with The Dwelling Co. and Wren & Holt. Those are the brands our target customers are also shopping. The Dwelling Co. in particular has done something remarkable with their visual identity — it's restrained but very considered.

**Diane Castellano:** We don't want to look like The Dwelling Co. — we want to be the version of that for someone who isn't paying $600 for a throw pillow. That's the sweet spot.

**Claire Whitmore:** That's our brief right there, honestly. Aspirational without alienating.

**Marcus Linde:** Jeff, from a channel standpoint, where are your customers actually finding you? I want to make sure we're building creative that fits the real journey.

**Jeff Purdy:** Pinterest is huge for us, surprisingly — way bigger than we'd expected. Instagram is important but it's more of a brand awareness play than a conversion channel for us. Email has historically been our highest ROI channel but the templates are honestly embarrassing at this point — they look like they were built in 2015 because they basically were.

**Diane Castellano:** We also have an OOH presence in three markets — Chicago, Atlanta, and Austin — that's coming up for renewal in September. We'd love to have new creative ready for that placement.

**Claire Whitmore:** Perfect. That actually aligns really well with what we were thinking for a campaign launch window. We're going to want to develop audience personas before we finalize any messaging direction. Jeff, would you be able to share any customer research you have? Segmentation data, purchase behavior, anything like that?

**Jeff Purdy:** Yes — I'll get you our 2023 customer segmentation report. We also did a brand perception study in Q1 of this year. Both are under NDA but I'll get legal to clear that with your team.

**Diane Castellano:** One thing I want to flag early: our board is very attached to the wordmark. We've had internal conversations about this. Any logo evolution needs to retain the logotype — we're not rebranding from scratch, we're refreshing. I don't want that to be a surprise.

**Claire Whitmore:** Noted, and that's totally workable. Tomás, our senior designer who'll be leading the logo work, is actually really skilled at evolution versus revolution. I think you'll be pleased.

**Marcus Linde:** Great. Let me just confirm action items before we close out. From your side: Jeff to send the 2023 segmentation report and Q1 brand perception study by May 17th. From our side: we'll have a formal proposal to you by May 14th, and Claire will send over a preliminary discovery questionnaire by end of this week for Diane and Jeff to complete async. Does that all track?

**Diane Castellano:** Works for me.

**Jeff Purdy:** Confirmed. Looking forward to seeing the proposal.

**Marcus Linde:** Perfect. Talk soon, everyone.
